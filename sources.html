External References

Literature & Theoretical Context

While this research is based on original observational data, the following existing frameworks and concepts informed our metric design and growth model selection:

  1. Superlinear scaling in networks: Bettencourt, L. M. A. (2013). "The Origins of Scaling in Cities." Science, 340(6139), 1438-1441. — Theoretical foundation for why network effects can produce superlinear returns.
  2. Recursive self-improvement: Yudkowsky, E. (2008). "Artificial Intelligence as a Positive and Negative Factor in Global Risk." Global Catastrophic Risks. — Early theoretical treatment of AI systems improving their own improvement processes.
  3. Collective intelligence metrics: Woolley, A. W., et al. (2010). "Evidence for a Collective Intelligence Factor in the Performance of Human Groups." Science, 330(6004), 686-688. — Measurement frameworks for group intelligence that inspired multi-agent metrics.
  4. Learning curve theory: Wright, T. P. (1936). "Factors Affecting the Cost of Airplanes." Journal of the Aeronautical Sciences, 3(4), 122-128. — Classic power-law learning curves used as baseline comparison models.

Novelty claim: While these prior works provide theoretical context, the quantitative measurement of recursive self-improvement events and superlinear collective learning in live multi-agent AI systems is, to our knowledge, unprecedented. This research contributes new empirical data and operational metrics, not new theory.
